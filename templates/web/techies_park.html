About Talrop

Techies Park is one of the business initiative by Talrop that serves as a community hub, being built in every assembly constituency of Kerala. Talrop focuses on Education, Technology, Jobs and Entrepreneurship to build a strong startup ecosystem in Kerala. Techies Parks is dedicated to achieving multiple goals including bridging the gap between industry and academy, fostering more entrepreneurship within the state, ensuring that technology is accessible in every corner of the state, creating numerous job opportunities, and attracting foreign investment from abroad. Each Techies Park is built by spending Rupees 1 Crore in every assembly constituencies.

01

The students from Jamia Nadwiyya campus who joined Techies Park were highly skilled and possessed a deep understanding of the local culture and challenges faced by startups in Kerala. Talrop succeeded in training engineers from this arabic college by teaching them coding alongside their regular studies.

02

The Techies Park offers a unique blend of programmes including industrial visits, workshops and technology awareness programmes for students, ICT training programme for teachers, cyber awareness programme for parents, e-literacy programme for locals and digital marketing programme for entrepreneurs.

03

Talrop has embarked on a mission that is set to transform Kerala's technological landscape. With a vision to build 140 Techies Parks by the end of 2025, Talrop is committed to changing the entire future of the state and put an end to its rising unemployment rate. These Techies Parks are not only significant for its economic impact.

The Techies Park in Jamia Nadwiyya campus was established in 2018 with the aim of bridging the industry-academic gap and providing the students of the college with the knowledge and tools they need to have a successful career. The BCA students of Jamia Nadwiyya campus spend half their day in college and the other half at Techies Park, which enables them to apply their knowledge into practice by using the facilities provided. With the help of Talrop and its Techies Park, Jamia Nadwiyya campus has become a hub for nurturing aspiring engineers.
